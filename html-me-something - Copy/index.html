Located an hour & a half outside of the bustling city of Puerto Vallarta in the State of Jalisco, sits , Carmen's Panaderia. Stepping out of the bus, the smell of fresh bread baking in the outdoor ovens and beans cooking over on open firepit filled the air. A look around the Hacienda was a delight for the eyes!
We found hidden gems in the gardens overlooking the gorge, became nostalgic upon entering the children's garden area complete with swing and a dollhouse, started sweating at the sight of so many chilies spread on the ground to dry, and were tickled by the moral laden, humorous tale of a mug with a broken ear shared by Carmen's jaunty family member. Carmen's has been in this location for many years. Carmen's has been the last stop (or first if traveling out of the mountains) before venturing down the very narrow, less than one lane dirt road and back up to the other side. The old road down in and out of the ravine was treacherous and travel was condition dependant. Since the road, in most places, allowed for only 1 vehicle at a time with minimal areas to wait for oncoming traffic to pass, the journey could take hours, luckily Carmen's Panaderia was there to provide a cool place to rest, grab a drink and a snack and soothe weary travelers and tourists before proceeding onward.
